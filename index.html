About

Derek Cantrell

Derek Cantrell is a high school principal at Alleghany High School in Virginia and the 2026 Virginia Association of Secondary School Principals (VASSP) Outstanding High School Principal of the Year.

  • High school principal at Alleghany High School
  • Author of Better Together
  • Speaker and consultant
  • 2026 VASSP Outstanding High School Principal of the Year

He is a recognized leader in school culture, instructional innovation, and leading through change. With nine years of experience as a principal, he has played a key role in the successful consolidation of two rival high schools into one unified school community, building a strong and positive culture centered on connection, collaboration, and shared success.

Under his leadership, Alleghany High School has implemented impactful initiatives focused on student engagement, staff recognition, and academic improvement. His work emphasizes relationships, clear systems, and intentional culture-building to drive both academic and organizational success.

Derek is a frequent presenter at state and national conferences, including the Virginia Association of Secondary School Principals (VASSP) and the Making Schools Work Conference in New Orleans. He has also been featured on education leadership podcasts, including the Proud Principal Podcast, Principal Matters, and Better Leaders Better Schools.

In addition to his leadership work, Derek is an author and speaker focused on helping schools navigate change while maintaining a strong, people-centered culture. His message centers on the idea that schools are strongest when they operate with a shared vision and a commitment to being Better Together.

Better Together Philosophy

People. Systems. Consistency.

Culture is not one more thing on a leader's plate. It is the plate. Better Together Consulting helps teams turn shared values into repeatable leadership habits that make people feel seen, supported, and ready to grow.

People

Build trust through relationships, visibility, listening, and moments that help staff and students feel noticed and valued.

Systems

Create clear routines for communication, meetings, recognition, data conversations, and instructional follow-through.

Consistency

Reinforce what matters daily so culture is taught, modeled, celebrated, and sustained beyond a single event.

The Book

Better Together: A Leadership Playbook for Culture, Change, and Unity

Lesson Plan A

Teacher: Mrs. Ashley Roberts
Course: Algebra I
Lesson Length: 90 Minutes

Virginia SOL: A.4 - Solve systems of linear equations in two variables using graphing and algebraic methods.

Learning Target: I can solve systems of equations by graphing and explain why the point of intersection represents the solution.

Essential Question: How can multiple methods be used to determine whether two relationships share the same solution?

Success Criteria:

  • Solve systems using graphing.
  • Explain mathematical reasoning.
  • Compare multiple solution methods.
  • Defend answers using evidence.

Bell Ringer: Students analyze two graphs and predict where they will intersect. Students complete a Turn and Talk discussing: "What do you notice?" and "What do you wonder?"

Direct Instruction (I Do): Teacher models solving one system using Desmos while thinking aloud. Students predict each next step before it is completed.

Guided Practice (We Do): Students solve progressively more difficult systems with partners. Teacher circulates asking probing questions instead of providing answers.

Collaborative Learning (You Do Together): Groups solve a real-world ticket sales problem. Students create chart paper explaining their mathematical process, supporting evidence, and why their solution works. Groups participate in a gallery walk and provide peer feedback.

Checks for Understanding:

  • Whiteboards
  • Poll Everywhere
  • Teacher questioning
  • Peer feedback
  • Exit Ticket

Differentiation Supports: Graphic organizers, color-coded graphs, and sentence stems.

Extensions: Three-variable systems and students create their own real-world application.

Assessment: Students complete an exit ticket requiring them to solve, explain, and defend reasoning.

Reflection: Students complete "I used to think..." and "Now I think..."

Quick Reflection
  • What strengths did the GPT identify?
  • Was there anything you had not considered?
  • What suggestions could make an already strong lesson even better?

Lesson Plan B

Teacher: Mr. Jason Miller

Virginia SOL: A.4

Sample Assessment

Algebra I Quiz
Virginia SOL A.4
Systems of Linear Equations

Directions: Answer all questions. Show your work where appropriate.

  1. Solve the following system by graphing: y = 2x + 3 and y = -x + 6. (4 points)
  2. Solve the system algebraically: 2x + y = 8 and x - y = 1. (4 points)
  3. What is the definition of a system of linear equations? (2 points)
  4. Circle the point where the two lines intersect. Graph provided. A. (2,1) B. (1,4) C. (3,5) D. (0,2). (2 points)
  5. Which of the following is an example of slope? A. x + y = 4 B. y = 3x + 2 C. (4,5) D. x^2 + y^2 = 9. (2 points)
  6. A concert sold adult tickets for $12 and student tickets for $8. 150 total tickets were sold for $1,520. Write and solve a system of equations to determine how many adult and student tickets were sold. Show all work. (6 points)
  7. Explain why the point of intersection represents the solution to a system of equations. Use mathematical evidence to support your answer. (4 points)
  8. Which solving method is ALWAYS the best method? A. Graphing B. Substitution C. Elimination D. There is always one correct method. (2 points)
  9. True or False: A system of equations always has exactly one solution. (1 point)
  10. Draw a picture that represents two intersecting lines. (2 points)
  11. Extra Credit: Write three interesting facts about famous mathematicians. (+3 points)
Follow-Up Prompt #1

Identify which questions are well aligned to the Virginia SOL and which questions should be revised or removed.

Explain your reasoning for each.

Follow-Up Prompt #2

Rewrite this assessment so every question aligns to the intended Virginia SOL and includes an appropriate balance of procedural fluency, conceptual understanding, application, and mathematical reasoning.

Follow-Up Prompt #3

Classify each assessment question using Webb's Depth of Knowledge (DOK).

Explain why each question fits that level and identify where additional rigor is needed.
